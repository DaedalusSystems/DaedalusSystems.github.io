Process

From messy tools to a steady system

Every engagement is custom, but the shape is usually the same: map what exists, build carefully, then refine based on how you actually work.

1
Map & observe
Roughly 1–2 short calls and a few shared screenshots.

We walk through how leads arrive today, where they get lost, and which tools you want to keep. The outcome is a clear sketch of your current flow and a shortlist of bottlenecks.

2
Design & build
Focused on the smallest meaningful first system.

Talos is wired between your existing tools to fix the most painful gaps first — often intake, first response, and basic tagging. You see and approve the language and timing before anything goes live.

3
Refine & extend
Adjustments over weeks, not a one-time install.

As real leads move through the system, we tune the pacing, add or remove steps, and layer in additional automations where they make sense. The goal is a calm, durable system, not a flashy launch.

Example flows

What this looks like in real days

These are typical patterns, not promises — but they show the kind of weight Talos can carry without stepping on your voice.

New portal inquiry
From “just came in” to organized lead
  • Lead arrives from a portal and is pushed into your CRM with source and price range tagged.
  • A short, warm message goes out in your tone with one or two simple questions.
  • If they reply, the automation pauses and you continue the conversation yourself.
  • If they do not, Talos schedules a gentle follow-up for later — no spreadsheet needed.
Open house signup
From paper list to next steps
  • Names and emails from an open house form are pulled into your CRM the same day.
  • Attendees receive a brief thank-you with a recap link or key details.
  • Hot leads are flagged for a same-day call; others are added to a light nurture path.
  • Your next morning starts with a short list of people to personally reach out to.
Long-term nurture
Staying present without hovering
  • “Someday” buyers are grouped by timeline and interest instead of one big bucket.
  • Every so often, they receive a brief, useful check-in that still sounds like you.
  • Replies move them into a more active follow-up path; silence does not flood them.
  • You see a concise overview of who is warming up before it feels urgent.
